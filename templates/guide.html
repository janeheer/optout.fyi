Know your rights

The California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), grants privacy rights to California residents. However, many companies process privacy requests broadly rather than risk non-compliance, and many states now have similar privacy laws. Sending a CCPA-style request creates a paper trail and puts a company on notice even if you are not a California resident.

Most AI surveillance companies meet the CCPA thresholds: over $25 million in annual revenue, or buying, selling, or sharing data on 100,000 or more consumers. When they receive a valid request, they are required to respond within the applicable deadline.

Key deadlines

  • 45 calendar days: Right to Know, Delete, Correct, and Opt Out of Profiling.
  • 15 business days: Opt Out of Sale/Sharing and Limit Sensitive Personal Information.
  • Companies may request one 45-day extension, but they must notify you within the initial response period.

If a company fails to respond within the deadline, you can file a complaint with the California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA) and/or the California Attorney General. Civil penalties can reach up to $2,500 per violation, or $7,500 per intentional violation.

How to send your letter

Option 1: Email

Use the company's privacy email address and keep a copy in your sent folder as proof.

Option 2: Privacy portal

Some companies require submission through an online portal. Paste the letter into the form and save screenshots.

Option 3: Certified mail

Certified mail creates the clearest proof of delivery and gives you a physical record of timing.

How to file a complaint

Step 1: File with the CPPA

The CPPA has independent enforcement authority and can impose fines of $2,500 to $7,500 per violation.

CPPA complaint form

Step 2: File with the California Attorney General

The Attorney General has concurrent enforcement authority and may use complaints in pattern-of-practice investigations.

Attorney General complaint form

Step 3: Keep records

Save your original letter, proof of delivery, screenshots, complaint drafts, and any company responses. Keep dates organized.
